National Bacon Day

National Bacon Day, celebrated annually on December 30, is a sizzling occasion dedicated to one of the world's favorite breakfast staples. Whether crispy or chewy, bacon's rich, savory flavor has earned it a special place in culinary history and modern kitchens alike. Let’s explore its history, fun ways to celebrate, and a few surprising facts about this beloved food.

A Brief but Delicious History of Bacon

Bacon’s origins trace back thousands of years. Ancient cultures, including the Chinese, preserved pork belly with salt to create early forms of bacon. The practice spread to Europe, where it evolved into the smoked and cured varieties we enjoy today. Fast forward to 1997, when National Bacon Day was created by bacon lovers as a lighthearted holiday to brighten the post-Christmas season.

Celebrating National Bacon Day

Bacon Day offers endless ways to indulge:

  • Cook Up Creative Dishes: Try bacon-wrapped appetizers, maple-bacon donuts, or even bacon infused cocktails.
  • Host a Bacon Party: Share the joy by serving bacon-themed dishes to friends and family.
  • Support Local Bacon Businesses: Visit restaurants or cafes offering unique bacon-based meals!
  • Try Something New: Experiment with plant-based bacon for a healthier, eco-friendly twist.
Fun Facts About Bacon
  • The average American consumes about 18 pounds of bacon yearly.
  • There are many bacon-inspired products; bacon-scented candles, bandages, cologne, and even soap!
  • The longest strip of bacon ever cooked measured over 3,000 feet!

This National Bacon Day, let’s savor the crispy, smoky goodness of bacon and enjoy the holiday spirit it brings. Whether you're a purist or adventurous eater, there’s a bacon experience for everyone to enjoy.
