International Tango Day

Ignite your passion on National Tango Day, where Argentina's sultry dance takes centre stage! Experience the magic of tango as it weaves together captivating rhythms, deep emotions, and cultural heritage, inviting you to join a global celebration that transcends borders and speaks the universal language of dance!

National Tango Day is a vibrant celebration of Argentina's most beloved dance form, honouring its captivating movements and rich cultural heritage. This day not only showcases the tango's ability to convey deep emotions and stories but also highlights its power to connect people across the globe, transcending language and borders.

History of National Tango Day

National Tango Day was established to honour two iconic figures in Argentine tango: Carlos Gardel and Julio De Caro, who share a birthday. The idea originated from Ben Molar, a passionate composer, who proposed an official day to celebrate tango's significance in Argentine culture. After gaining approval from the Buenos Aires government in the 1970s, December 11 was designated as National Tango Day, allowing people worldwide to appreciate this expressive dance form.

Today, National Tango Day not only celebrates the music and dance but also embraces the rich cultural history associated with tango, bringing people together in joy and connection.
