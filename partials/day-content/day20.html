International Human Solidarity Day, observed annually on 20 December, is a powerful global celebration of unity, diversity, and collective compassion. This remarkable day encourages people worldwide to recognise our shared humanity and work together to address global challenges through mutual understanding and support.

Historical Background

The UN established the World Solidarity Fund in 2002, with a mission to eradicate poverty and promote social development, particularly among the most vulnerable populations. In 2005, the UN officially proclaimed 20 December as International Human Solidarity Day, recognising solidarity as a fundamental universal value for 21st-century international relations.
